Structure of a Policy Brief
Title: Direct and descriptive of the issue.
Executive Summary: A 100-200 word summary highlighting the issue, key findings, and main recommendations.
Context/Background: Explain the problem, why it is critical now, and its relevance to current government priorities.
Evidence/Findings: Present key research or data, avoiding academic jargon.
Policy Options/Recommendations: Offer practical, action-oriented solutions, often listing pros and cons for each, justified by your findings.
Contact Details: Author biography and contact info for follow-up. 

Top Tips for UK Policy Audiences
Be Solution-Focused: Actively present solutions, not just problems.
Use Imperative Language: Use direct, authoritative language for recommendations (e.g., "Implement," "Reduce," "Establish").
Be Timely: Ensure the brief arrives before key decisions are made.
Visualise Data: Use charts, figures, or diagrams to break up text and explain complex info quickly.
Avoid Jargon: Write for a non-specialist audience.
Keep it Short: Maximum of 2–4 pages; use bullet points and bold key points for "skimmers". 

Reference Checklist:
Is it evidence-based? Use robust data.
Is it tailored? Address a specific, named audience.
Is it formatted for speed? Use headings and subheadings.
